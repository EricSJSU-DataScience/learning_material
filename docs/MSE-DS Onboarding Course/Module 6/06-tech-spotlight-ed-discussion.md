# Tech Spotlight: Ed Discussion

![Ed Discussion logo](ed-discussion-logo.png)

## What is Ed Discussion?

Ed Discussion is an online discussion forum where students, TAs and faculty can communicate and ask questions and support each other throughout the course. This forum is for students to discuss course-related topics and seek guidance for any questions they have about the material. Note that Ed Discussion is not meant for course feedback, but if you want to give feedback, you can instead use one of the many feedback channels provided by Penn Engineering Online (see the previous Course Feedback reading for more information).

Ed Discussion also allows students to communicate privately with Instructors. Being able to post privately allows students the ability to post their questions to the course team in a more one on one setting. Being able to post privately also allows students the ability to share code with TAs while not sharing it with other students.

---

## How will I use Ed Discussion?

As a student, you will use Ed Discussion as a place to communicate with other students as well as your TAs and Course Instructor.

The course staff will post announcements here so be sure to check in frequently!

Please be advised that sharing Ed Discussion Access Codes and Links with other students is AGAINST university policy. Only students actively enrolled in a course may access the Ed Discussion boards for that course. If you have questions or concerns, please reach out to the [Student Success team](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/connect-with-student-support/) for guidance.

## Ed Discussion Etiquette

Follow these rules to keep Ed Discussion organized and efficient.

### When Posting

- Submit a **Question** when you need an answer, but submit a **Post** when you don't need an answer but just want to post information.

- If a course staff member answers your question, but you need more information, uncheck the green check mark on your question so it remains unresolved, and then add a comment to your question.

- If you select **Anonymous**, you are anonymous to other students, but you are **not** anonymous to the instructors.

- When referring to another post, use "#" and the number of the post.

- All posts must be connected to a folder. These allow for easy organizing and searching.

- If the topic within a thread changes, start a new post.

### When Searching for Content

- You can search by keywords, folder, or by post number using "#."

### Best Practices

- If you have additional information, don't add it to the answer. Instead, edit the original request.

- Check whether there is a similar post before creating a new one.

- Be careful about accidentally revealing solutions.

- Do not post code publicly.

- Be courteous to your fellow students and TAs.

## Ed Discussion Lessons Learned from Our Students

**"Skim Ed Discussion regularly"** so it does not pile up and become overwhelming; this allows you to anticipate questions you may have, and you might get to a point on your homework where you say, "Oh, I saw that question already on Ed Discussion."

**"Connect with other students through Slack"** - they usually can point you to where your question is in Ed Discussion.

**"Frame your question to be about "why" and "how:"** instead of, "Did I do this right?" try for something like "Why do we use the Von Neumann model?" or "walk me through how you would decide whether to apply the permutation or combination formula."

"Prioritize, prioritize, prioritize. **Focus on course content (reading and videos) first, take a look at assignment(s) and Slack, and then skim Ed Discussion.** Try to respond to class questions (in your own head or by actually typing out a response) - it's a good exercise in checking your understanding once you've gone through the material."

**"There are typically filters along the left sidebar,** organized by assignment, exam, announcements, etc. Those can be helpful for filtering and searching through related posts."

"The same way you set aside time for hw, recitations, **set aside Ed Discussion time**. It's a chance to learn something from colleagues' questions, and perhaps save you a lot of time (or even the wrong answer) trying to interpret a question on a quiz or hw."

"TAs mainly look for unanswered questions and unresolved posts when answering Ed Discussion posts. Once a student responds to your question in the student answer box, the question appears as resolved so the TAs might not see it anymore. **If you still want an Instructor response, make an unresolved follow-up on the post**."
