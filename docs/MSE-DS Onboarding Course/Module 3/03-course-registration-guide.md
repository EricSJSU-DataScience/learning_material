# Course Registration Guide

Now that you've completed the Onboarding course, it's time to register for your first class or classes! This guide walks you through the registration process, important deadlines, and policies.

---

## Registration Overview

**Students should always register for courses prior to the beginning of each semester.** Course registration occurs through Path@Penn, where you'll search for courses, read descriptions, and add/drop courses during the registration period.

---

## Important Registration Deadlines

### Deferral Deadline

**Two weeks before the start of your first semester**, you must have a course registered or your account will be affected.

**Critical consequence:** Students who are NOT registered for courses or DROP their courses after the deferral deadline will be required to take a **Leave of Absence in their first semester**.

**Note:** If the onboarding course is not completed by the deadline, a registration hold may be placed on your account.

---

## Step-by-Step Course Registration

### 1. Log In to Path@Penn

Navigate to [Path@Penn](https://path.at.upenn.edu/) and log in with your Penn credentials.

### 2. Navigate to Academic Planning and Registration

Look for the section titled **"Academic Planning and Registration"** on your dashboard. Within that box, select **"Search and Register for classes."**

### 3. Access the Course Search Tool

You should now see the **"Search Classes"** tool. The Course Search interface will appear with options to filter and search for courses.

### 4. Select Your Semester and Search

In the **left sidebar**:
- Select which semester you want to register for at the top
- Enter the exact course number you're looking for, or type in 'CIT' or 'CIS' to browse courses
- Note: Some electives are offered by different departments (e.g., ESE)

![alt text](03_image1.png)

### 5. Filter for Online Courses

To view only online courses:
- Select the box **"Find courses available to students in online programs"**, OR
- When browsing, look for course numbers ending with section **501** (e.g., CIT 5920-501)
- The 501 section number indicates it's an Engineering Online course

### 6. Review Course Descriptions

- Select the course number to read its full description
- Check prerequisites, learning outcomes, and schedule

### 7. Add Courses to Cart

Once you've found a course you want:
- In the **bottom right corner**, select **"Add to cart"**
- Add the course to your **Primary Cart**

### 8. Review Your Primary Cart

In the **left sidebar**, navigate to **"Primary Cart"**:
- You should see all courses you've added
- Review your selections
- Make any changes if necessary

### 9. Submit Your Schedule

Once you've finalized your selections:
- Click **"Submit schedule"**
- Your courses will appear in your Primary Cart

### 10. Confirm Successful Registration

After clicking "Submit Schedule":
- A **black check mark** next to your course indicates successful registration
- If the symbol does not appear, follow the instructions in Path or contact your advisor

## Add and Drop Courses

### Add Course Deadline

You may add courses until the deadline listed on the [Penn Engineering Online Calendar](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/degrees/student-services/academic-calendar/).

**Important:** The University academic calendar dates do not always apply to online students. **Always refer to the Penn Engineering Online calendar instead.**

### Drop Course Deadlines

**Drop (100% Refund)**
- Drop a course **without financial obligation** up until the drop deadline
- Tuition will automatically be **100% refunded** if dropped before this date

**Late Drop (50% Refund)**
- After the drop deadline, you may **late drop** for a **50% refund** of tuition
- Late drop deadline is listed on the Penn Engineering Online Calendar

**Important Restriction:** In fall and spring terms, you may **not drop a course if it is your only registered course**. In this case, you would need to request a **Leave of Absence** (or for your first term, a **deferral**).

## Withdrawing from a Course

### When You Can Withdraw

After the **final Drop Deadline** has passed, you may request to **withdraw** from a course by contacting program staff.

### Course Withdrawal Process

Students must complete the following steps:

1. **Submit the Course Withdrawal form** via the [Student Knowledge Base](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/)
2. **Schedule an appointment with your Academic Advisor** via the [Knowledge Base](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/)
   - Attending an advising session is **required** as part of the withdrawal process

3. Penn Engineering Online office approves the request
4. University Registrar's office finalizes the request
5. You'll receive email confirmation when withdrawal is complete
6. A **"W"** will appear on your transcript

### Financial and Academic Impact

- **No refund** issued for Course Withdrawal (you are responsible for tuition payment)
- **Does NOT affect** your GPA calculation
- **Before withdrawing:** If you're on financial aid, consult with your financial aid counselor to discuss how this will affect your aid package

### Course Withdrawal Limit

**Important limit:** Penn Engineering Online students may **withdraw ("W") no more than three times** from any courses in the program.

- Maximum of three "W" grades on your transcript
- Excessive withdrawals impede academic progress
- Students with three or more "W" grades are subject to policies in the Student Handbook's **"Academic Standing Requirements" section**

## Your Registered Courses

### Viewing Your Courses

Your registered courses will appear in:
- Your **student profile** on the [Path@Penn landing page](https://path.at.upenn.edu/student/landing)
- Your **degree homepage in Canvas** starting on the first day of classes

### Unlocking Canvas Courses

- Canvas course links are **unlocked on the first day of classes**
- You'll receive access via your email once courses begin

---

## Important Notes

- **Limited enrollment:** Electives will have limited enrollment when first offered; other courses may have capped enrollment on a case-by-case basis
- **Waitlist instructions:** The program will send instructions on how to add your name to waitlists if enrollment is capped
- **Enrollment changes:** Always monitor your email for program communications about course availability

---

## Key Points

- Register for courses before the deferral deadline (two weeks before semester start) or face leave of absence requirements
- Use Path@Penn to search, add, and register for courses
- Always refer to the Penn Engineering Online Calendar, not the general University calendar, for deadlines
- Drop courses by the deadline for full (100%) or late-drop deadline for partial (50%) refund
- Withdraw from courses only after the drop deadline; no refund issued
- Cannot drop your only registered course in fall/spring; must request leave of absence instead
- Maximum of three course withdrawals allowed; excess may affect academic standing
- Canvas course links unlock on the first day of classes
- Always check your Penn email for important registration announcements and deadlines
