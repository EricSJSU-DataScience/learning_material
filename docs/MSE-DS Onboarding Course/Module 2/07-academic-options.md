# Academic Options

MSE-DS Online offers several academic options to accommodate diverse student backgrounds and experiences. This page explains transfer credit policies and competency exam options.

---

## Transfer Credits

### Eligibility

You may transfer up to **two courses** (with faculty approval) to fulfill program requirements.

**Important timeline:** Transfer courses must be submitted **within your first academic year** (one full cycle of fall, spring, and summer) at Penn Engineering Online.

**Key point:** Course credit is transferred, not the course grade. Transfer credits do not impact your GPA.

### Transfer Course Requirements

Courses must meet ALL of the following criteria to be approved:

- **Grade:** B or higher
- **Level:** Graduate-level courses taken as part of a Master's or PhD degree
- **Timing:** Courses must be completed **prior to matriculation** at Penn Engineering Online
- **Recency:** Courses must be taken **within five (5) years** from the start of your Penn Engineering Online degree program

### Transfer Course Selection

- You must select a **Penn Engineering equivalent course** for each transfer
- General elective credit cannot be granted
- Transfer requests are evaluated **one-to-one** (one transfer course to one Penn course)
- Requests to combine multiple courses to satisfy one Penn course will **not be approved**

### Requesting Transfer Credit

#### Deadlines and Instructions

Transfer credit request deadlines and signup instructions will be **emailed to you**. Keep a close lookout for these communications.

#### Required Information

Include the following with your transfer request:

- **Year taken** (must be within the past 5 years)
- **Course number and title**
- **Institution name** (must be graduate-level from a graduate program—no exceptions)
- **Letter grade received** (must be B or higher)
- **Target MSE-DS course** - Identify which MSE-DS Online course you're seeking credit for

#### Required Attachments

- Course syllabus
- Unofficial transcript
- **If applicable:** If your course does not use letter grades A-F, include documentation of the institution's grading scheme and how grades are determined

#### Decision Timeline

- **Preliminary decision:** May be sent before classes start (not final)
- **Final decision:** After this semester's drop deadline
- You will receive an official decision after the drop deadline passes

### Dual Degree Students

If you are a Dual Degree Student, you will transfer in **4 double-counting CUs** from your MCIT/MAS-CS Online degree.

**To apply:** Send your plan of study to have these transfer credits applied.

## CIT 5950 Competency Exam

### Purpose

The CIT 5950 Competency Exam allows MSE-DS Online students to **enroll in courses with CIT 5950 as a prerequisite** without taking the full CIT 5950 course.

**Who should consider this:** Students with prior experience covering all learning material taught in CIT 5950.

### Eligibility Example

You may be a strong candidate if you have:
- Written a C program at least 200 lines long
- Used GDB and Valgrind
- Used Docker
- Written a multi-threaded network program in C

### Competency Exam Deadlines and Signup

Deadlines and instructions to sign up will be **emailed to you**. Keep a close lookout for these communications.

### Exam Policies

**Availability**
- Only available during a **specified window in the middle of the semester**
- Cannot be offered on an individualized schedule

**Enrollment**
- You may **not enroll in electives requiring CIT 5950** until you successfully complete this competency exam

**Attempt Limit**
- You may **only attempt this exam once** during your program
- Choose carefully—you have only one opportunity

**Timeline**
- **Five weeks from start to finish** to complete screening and the three-part project
- **Extensions are NOT available**

**Passing Requirements**
- Must score **90% or higher on each component** of the three-part project
- All three components must meet this threshold to pass

**Study Materials**
- **No access to CIT 5950 course content** is provided during the competency exam
- The exam is designed to assess if your prior experience has prepared you with equivalent knowledge of CIT 5950 learning outcomes
- This is not a self-study or teaching tool; it is a proficiency assessment

## Key Points

- Transfer up to 2 courses within your first academic year (with faculty approval)
- Transfer credits do not impact GPA; only the credit transfers
- Courses must be graduate-level, B grade or higher, completed within 5 years
- Submit transfer requests with syllabus, transcript, and grading documentation
- Final transfer decision comes after the drop deadline each semester
- Dual Degree students automatically transfer 4 double-counting CUs from MCIT/MAS-CS Online
- CIT 5950 Competency Exam allows bypassing prerequisite if you have relevant experience
- Competency exam is only available during set window in middle of semester
- You have one attempt only; must score 90%+ on all three components to pass
- Five-week completion window; no extensions available
- Competency exam is assessment only—no course materials provided
