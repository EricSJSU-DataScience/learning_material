# Planning Your First Semester and Beyond

Thoughtful course planning is an important practice that will contribute to your success as a student at Penn Engineering Online. In this section, you'll learn how to plan your first semester and map out your entire degree.

---

## First Semester Course Load

### Recommended Course Load

We **strongly encourage you to take no more than two courses in your first semester**. This is a rigorous program, often requiring students to dedicate an average of **15+ hours per week to each course** (note that 15 hours is an average and can vary widely).

**Important:** If considering 3 courses in your first semester, we strongly recommend meeting with an advisor beforehand to discuss your schedule.

### Why Start Small?

Taking one or two courses rather than a full-time load can give you:
- Time and space to adjust to the online learning environment
- An opportunity to adapt to program expectations if you're new to online learning
- Better balance if you have full-time commitments outside the program

[Reach out to your Academic Advisor](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/connect-with-student-support/) to course plan with support.

## Academic Coaches

Your introductory courses will assign you an **Academic Coach**—a current student or alumni who serves as a trusted peer to new students.

### What Academic Coaches Do

- Meet regularly with students during their transition into the degree
- Offer personalized guidance, mentoring, and tutoring
- Empower you to succeed and thrive in the program
- Help you develop the tools and confidence to achieve your goals

### How to Connect

Your Academic Coach will email you and add you to their Slack channel at the start of the semester. Sign up for their office hours and attend information sessions throughout the semester as needed!

## Planning Beyond Your First Semester

### Course Selection Guidelines

You can choose any courses offered so long as you:
- Meet the prerequisites or corequisites
- The course is a requirement within the MSE-DS curriculum

**Important Note:** When you see CIT 5910, 5920, 5930, or 5940 listed as prerequisites, **these are waived for MSE-DS Online students**.

To map out your plan, we encourage all students to **build a plan of study**.

---

## What is a Plan of Study?

A plan of study is an outline of every course you plan to take from your first semester through your last semester. Build your own plan using the [Plan of Study Builder Template](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1ydfDyzeGmWFB1GU2tIqy8PkhlVbor60L/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=103414948889862807004&rtpof=true&sd=true).

---

## Why a Plan of Study Matters

A plan of study:
1. Gives you ownership of your academic journey
2. Outlines the roadmap to achieve your goals
3. Allows you to navigate unexpected obstacles
4. Reduces academic planning confusion and stress
5. Helps communicate your plans with your Academic Advisor

---

## How to Build Your Plan of Study

### Questions to Ask Yourself

- When are the courses I want to take typically offered?
- Do I meet the prerequisites or corequisites for this course?
- Can I fit this course into a future term if I don't get in now?
- Am I completing my degree within the allotted time?

## Key Points

- Take no more than 2 courses in your first semester (expect 15+ hours per week per course)
- Start with one of the recommended foundational courses
- Connect with your assigned Academic Coach early
- Use free Penn Engineering prep courses to refresh skills
- Build a comprehensive plan of study for your entire degree
- Use the provided resources to research course schedules and prerequisites
- CIT prerequisites (5910, 5920, 5930, 5940) are waived for MSE-DS students
- Your Academic Advisor is available to help with course planning
- Course schedule is always subject to change—plan flexibly
