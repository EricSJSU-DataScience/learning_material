# What To Expect In Your MSE-DS Online Courses

This page outlines what you can expect from the MSE-DS Online program, how it differs from other online learning platforms, and how to prepare yourself for success.

---

## How MSE-DS Online Differs from MOOCs

You may have taken online courses through Coursera, Edx, or other platforms before. **MSE-DS Online is fundamentally different from Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs).**

### Key Differences

**Scale & Selectivity**
- MOOCs: Tens of thousands of free participants
- MSE-DS: Few hundred admitted, competitive degree program
- You receive a Master's degree from University of Pennsylvania upon graduation

**Course Structure**
- **Deadlines are firm** - You must follow the course schedule
- **Assignments are more rigorous** - Higher difficulty and expectations
- **Fixed submission schedules** - No flexibility to set your own deadline
- **Extensive support** - Team of TAs, course managers, and success advisors supporting every step

**Support & Engagement**
- Office hours and recitations available
- Discussion board questions answered within 24 hours
- One-on-one help from trained professionals
- Peer interaction encouraged and facilitated

**Content Depth**
- MOOCs cover breadth; MSE-DS goes deep
- Rigorous curriculum designed to upgrade skills
- Content shapes your ability to work with emerging technologies

## Time Commitment & Course Load

### Realistic Time Expectations

The MSE-DS program requires substantial time investment:

- **Average per course:** 15+ hours per week
- **Some weeks:** 20-25 hours for challenging assignments
- **Note:** 15 hours is an average and can vary widely

### Before the Semester Starts

Conduct a thought exercise: Map out the extra time you typically have each week and honestly assess whether you can dedicate 15+ hours to one class.

**Ask yourself:**
- What if this course takes 20-25 hours some weeks?
- Can I sustain that while managing work and personal life?
- How many courses can I realistically handle?

### First Semester Recommendations

**We strongly recommend taking 1-2 courses your first semester.**

This allows you to:
- Adjust to the rigorous structure
- Master new material without overwhelming yourself
- Adapt to the online format
- Develop good study habits and time management
- Gauge how many courses you can handle going forward

**You can always increase your course load later** if you find the first semester manageable. Talk to an academic advisor about what's right for your situation.

## Engagement and Success

### Active Participation is Key

**Interact with instructors, TAs, and fellow students.** While courses are asynchronous, the teaching staff is eager to work with you.

Reach out through:
- Office hours
- Discussion boards
- Slack workspace
- Direct email to instructors

**It's okay to ask for help.** The staff expects and welcomes questions.

### Balancing Individual and Group Work

Throughout the semester, you'll have opportunities to:
- Work on projects in and out of the classroom with other students
- Collaborate on assignments
- Build connections with your cohort
- Enhance your learning through peer interaction

**Don't be passive.** Meet other students and start conversations early.

### Assignments AND Exams Matter

Both components significantly contribute to your grade:
- Assignments are not "extra credit" or secondary
- Exams carry equal weight
- Complete and submit both for best results

## Special Considerations for International Students

If this is your first time learning from a U.S.-based Ivy League institution, your experience may differ from previous educational environments. Consider these tips from international students who have succeeded in the program:

**Academic Engagement**
- Actively interact with TAs and instructors
- Don't wait to struggle—ask questions early
- Participate in office hours and discussions
- Leverage the support available to you

**Understanding Expectations**
- Deadlines are firm; late work policies exist but are strict
- Assignments require significant time and effort
- Both homework and exams are essential
- Quality of work matters as much as completion

**Cultural Adaptation**
- The pace may be faster than you expect
- American classrooms value active participation
- Email communication is professional and direct
- Office hours are not just for crisis management—they're for learning

## Managing Challenges

### When Courses Get Tough

**You may feel overwhelmed or struggle.** This is normal. Many students experience challenging periods.

**You may also face personal challenges** that affect your coursework:
- Changes in job responsibilities
- Family situations
- Unexpected life events
- Health concerns

### Support is Available

**A trained team of professionals is here for you**, not just in courses but for whatever else you're facing.

Available support:
- Regular office hours with staff
- Email support (available to respond anytime)
- Academic Coaches for first-semester courses
- Student Success Advisors
- Academic Advisors for course planning

**Reach out early.** The sooner you communicate challenges, the more we can help.

## Key Points

- MSE-DS Online is **rigorous and structured**, not like free online MOOCs
- Expect **15+ hours per week per course**, sometimes 20-25 hours
- Take **1-2 courses your first semester** to adjust successfully
- **Firm deadlines** and formal structure are the norm
- **Active engagement** with instructors and peers is essential for success
- Both **assignments and exams** significantly impact your grade
- **Ask for help early**—staff is trained to support you
- Support extends beyond academics to personal challenges
- International students: this may differ from your previous educational experience
- You're joining a competitive, selective program with high-quality support
