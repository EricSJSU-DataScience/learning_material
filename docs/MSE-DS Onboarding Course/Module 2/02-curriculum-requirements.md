# MSE-DS Curriculum Requirements

To earn an MSE-DS Online degree, you'll complete ten (10) course units distributed across four categories. This page outlines the curriculum structure, graduation requirements, grading policies, and important academic guidelines.

---

## Degree Requirements

### Course Distribution

To earn your MSE-DS degree, you must complete:

- **3 Foundational Course Units** - Build core data science fundamentals
- **4 Core Course Units** - Develop essential data science skills
- **2 Technical Elective Units** - Specialize in areas of interest
- **1 Open Elective Unit** - Choose from any approved course

**Total: 10 Course Units**

### Course Sequencing

Students may take MSE-DS courses in any order so long as **prerequisites and corequisites are followed**. Plan your courses carefully to ensure you meet all requirements.

---

## Graduation Requirements

To graduate, you must:

1. **Meet course distribution requirements** - Complete all required course units in the categories above
2. **Maintain minimum GPA** - Achieve and maintain a **2.7 cumulative average**
3. **Resolve incomplete grades** - Cannot graduate with "I," "NR," or "GR" on transcript
4. **Pass all core courses** - No grade lower than "D" counts in core courses; must retake if needed

### Academic Performance Plan

Students with a cumulative grade below 2.7 at the end of each semester will work with an academic advisor to create a plan for improving their academic performance.

---

## Course Load Options

You may take **1-4 courses each semester** based on your schedule and work commitments:

- **Part-time:** 2.99 CUs or less per semester
- **Full-time:** 3+ CUs per semester
- **Half-time (Financial Aid):** 2 CUs per semester

## Grading System

### Penn Engineering GPA Scale

| Grade | GPA |
|-------|-----|
| A+ | 4.0 |
| A | 4.0 |
| A- | 3.7 |
| B+ | 3.3 |
| B | 3.0 |
| B- | 2.7 |
| C+ | 2.3 |
| C | 2.0 |
| C- | 1.7 |
| D+ | 1.3 |
| D | 1.0 |
| F | 0 |

### Grading Policies

**Course Grading Discretion**
The course instructor has full discretion regarding assignment and course grading policies. Refer to individual course syllabi for specific details.

**Grade Retakes**
- No course may be retaken to improve a grade of "D" or higher unless the content is different
- No grade lower than "D" counts in core courses; those courses must be retaken
- Graduation may not be postponed for grade improvement if all other graduation criteria are met
- Maximum of **two attempts** at the same course allowed
- All letter grades (A, B, etc.), including "W," "I," and "F" count as an attempt

---

## Special Grade Designations

**Incomplete ("I")**
Designates unfinished coursework. Cannot graduate with an "I" on transcript.

**No Report ("NR")**
Indicates no grade was submitted by instructor. Cannot graduate with "NR" on transcript.

**Grade Report Not Filed ("GR")**
Indicates student was not present in class though enrolled. Cannot graduate with "GR" on transcript.

**Failure ("F")**
Does not count toward course requirements. Core course failures must be retaken.

---

## Special Notes for Dual Degree Students

If you are a Dual Degree student, once you matriculate into the MSE-DS Online Program, you must complete the degree within **three years**. 

**Leave of Absence (LOA)**
- Maximum of two Fall/Spring semesters allowed for LOA
- LOAs and time for degree completion **do not carry over** from MCIT Online degree

**Review Resources**
Consult the Student Handbook for comprehensive Dual Degree MSE-DS Online curriculum requirements.

## Key Points

- MSE-DS requires 10 course units: 3 foundational, 4 core, 2 technical electives, 1 open elective
- Maintain a minimum 2.7 cumulative GPA to graduate
- Complete all graduation requirements or risk an incomplete degree
- You control your course load: take 1-4 courses per semester based on your situation
- Follow course prerequisites and corequisites when planning your schedule
- No grade of "D" or lower counts in core courses; those must be retaken
- Core course grades cannot be retaken for improvement; only if retaking the course itself
- Maximum of two attempts per course (including W, I, and F)
- Cannot graduate with incomplete grades (I, NR, GR) on transcript
- Dual Degree students must complete within three years of matriculation
