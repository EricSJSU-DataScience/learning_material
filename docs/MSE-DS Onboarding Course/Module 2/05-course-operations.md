# Course Operations and Support

This page outlines how MSE-DS Online courses operate, the support systems available to you, and the technical requirements you need to succeed.

---

## Course Schedule and Deadlines

### Module Structure

Course material is organized into modules, which are groups of lessons forming a larger unit of learning. Each module typically corresponds to one week in the course.

**Important Timeline:**
- Most or all lecture videos are available when the course opens
- Assignment due dates are staggered throughout the semester
- You can work ahead by watching videos, but course staff will prioritize answering questions about current week's content
- Questions about future week's content may take longer to answer

### Managing Deadlines

To stay on track:

1. **Check Canvas regularly** - Every time you log in, you'll see a list of upcoming due dates in your courses
2. **Review Assignments page** - Click on the Assignments page in each course to see the full deadline list
3. **Enable notifications** - Configure automated reminders in Canvas settings to get alerts about incomplete assignments
4. **Plan ahead** - Some assignments have prerequisite readings or videos due earlier in the week
5. **Manage your time** - Stay on top of deadlines and develop strategies to avoid falling behind

## Course Structure

### Course Materials

Your MSE-DS courses include:

- **Lecture materials** - Created by instructors to teach key concepts
- **Videos and resources** - Guest lectures, curated websites, and faculty-selected materials
- **Downloadable content** - Stream or download course material for offline viewing
  - **Important:** Downloaded material from Penn Engineering Online courses may not be distributed

### Course Activities

Throughout your courses, you'll engage with:

- **Regular assignments** - Quizzes, individual and group projects, coding assignments
- **Graded activities** - Synthesis and application of course concepts
- **Optional synchronous recitations** - Instructor-led review sessions
- **Course personality** - Each course varies based on the instructor's teaching style

## Office Hours and Recitations

### Open Office Hours

**Instructors offer open office hours weekly** where you can attend synchronously and ask questions.

- Links posted on Canvas
- Recordings uploaded to Canvas after sessions
- Optional, but highly recommended
- Direct interaction with professors and course staff
- Real-time problem-solving opportunities

**Benefits of attending (even occasionally):**
- Boost motivation
- Enhance your understanding
- Connect with peers
- Stay informed about new trends

### Private Office Hours

**Teaching Assistants offer private office hours** through our Waitwhile queuing system.

- One-on-one Zoom sessions with TAs (up to 15 minutes per student)
- Times posted on course calendar
- Not recorded
- Real-time support for specific questions

### Recitations

**Optional recitations are offered regularly** by TAs and/or Instructors.

- Demonstration of sample problems
- Supplemental materials
- Real-time Q&A
- Links posted on Canvas
- Recorded and posted to Canvas after completion

### Recording Notice

Recitations and open office hours are recorded and posted in the Class Recording section of Canvas.

- You can turn your camera off or on as you feel comfortable
- We encourage students to leave cameras on when possible to foster engagement

## Ed Discussion Forums

Ed Discussion is an online forum for course communication:

**Features:**
- Students, TAs, and faculty can communicate and support each other
- General forums for broad discussions
- Anonymous posting option available
- Private messaging option for sharing code with TAs without other students seeing it

**Response times:**
- Course staff respond within 24 hours
- Questions about future weeks may take longer to answer
- Fellow students may answer questions sooner

**More information** - A dedicated Tech Spotlight on Ed Discussion is covered in Module 6.

## Extension Policies

### Extenuating Circumstances

Extensions beyond the late policy are only granted for situations making it impossible to complete work on time.

**Approved reasons:**
- Medical issues and emergencies
- Death in the family
- Natural disasters
- [Secular and religious holidays](https://catalog.upenn.edu/pennbook/secular-religious-holidays/)

### Typically NOT Approved

- Work conflicts
- Weddings
- Vacations
- Multiple extension requests (if you need many extensions, contact the Student Success team)

### Extension Process

1. Submit requests through the form available on your course's Canvas home page
2. **Request at least 24 hours in advance** to be considered for approval
3. Review individual course syllabi for specific course details on extensions
4. For private questions, submit a ticket using the Contact Course Management form on Canvas

## Technical Requirements

### Internet Connection

**Your responsibility:**
- Maintain internet access for the duration of the program
- Ensure connection permits receiving and transmitting information freely
- Penn is not responsible for disruptions in your internet service

### Proctored Exams

A few times each semester, you'll take proctored exams using Honorlock.

- Review [Honorlock system requirements](https://honorlock.kb.help/minimum-system-requirements/) before exam day

- Installation of required software

## Key Points

- Courses are organized into weekly modules with staggered assignment due dates
- Use Canvas Assignments page and notification settings to track deadlines
- Take advantage of open office hours—they offer direct interaction with faculty and peers
- Private office hours with TAs provide one-on-one support (up to 15 minutes)
- Recitations are optional but valuable for concept review and real-time Q&A
- Ed Discussion Forums provide 24-hour response time from course staff
- All office hours and recitations are recorded for asynchronous viewing
- Extension requests require 24 hours advance notice and must cite extenuating circumstances
- Maintain reliable internet access and consider laptop requirements before starting
- Download and save course materials before courses conclude—access after completion is not guaranteed
- Support is available through multiple channels: office hours, discussions, email, and private messaging
