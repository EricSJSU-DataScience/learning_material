## University of Pennsylvania: Founded on Innovation

![Ben Franklin, Founder of the University of Pennsylvania](06_image1.png)

In 1749, Benjamin Franklin—printer, inventor, and future founding father of the United States—published his famous essay, *Proposals Relating to the Education of Youth*, circulated it among Philadelphia's leading citizens, and organized 24 trustees to form an institution of higher education that would become the University of Pennsylvania. Franklin advocated an innovative concept of higher education, one which simultaneously taught both the ornamental knowledge of the arts and the practical skills necessary for making a living.

More than 250 years after Ben Franklin broke new ground in founding Penn, its faculty, students, and alumni continue to make breakthroughs in research, scholarship, and education. Its many subsequent "firsts" include:

- The world's first electronic, large-scale, general-purpose digital computer ([ENIAC](http://www.seas.upenn.edu/about-seas/eniac/), 1946)
- The first woman president of an Ivy League institution ([Judith Rodin](https://archives.upenn.edu/exhibits/penn-history/institutional-planning/rodin/), inaugurated in 1994)
- The first female Ivy League president to succeed another female ([Amy Gutmann](https://almanac.upenn.edu/archive/volumes/v51/n08/pdf_n08/inaug.pdf), inaugurated in 2004)

Penn's proud tradition of translating knowledge into social-minded action, articulated in Franklin's maxim "well-done is better than well-said," lives today through our policies, innovative work, and impactful engagement of our faculty, students, and staff.

**Learn more:** [Penn's Heritage](https://www.upenn.edu/about/history) and [Penn's Traditions](https://osa.universitylife.upenn.edu/campus-traditions/)

## Penn Engineering and the Computer and Information Science Department

Penn Engineering is the birthplace of the modern computer. It was here that the ENIAC, the world's first electronic, large-scale, general-purpose digital computer, was developed in 1946. Since this auspicious beginning more than seven decades ago, the field of computer science at Penn has been marked by exciting innovations.

### Department Overview

Today, the Computer and Information Science department is home to:

- Nine research centers and institutes
- More than 50 world-class faculty
- Teaching and interdisciplinary research across many traditional and cutting-edge fields

### Research Areas

Faculty and students engage in research across:

- Intelligent systems and artificial intelligence
- Information systems and data science
- Hardware and software systems
- Computer networks
- Theory of computation

### Faculty and Students Impact

Faculty and students work together and across sectors to develop new ideas and solutions to challenges rapidly emerging in the field. Now, more than ever, breakthroughs in computer and information science are changing our world, and computer scientists have an amazing opportunity to lead that change.

**Explore Further:** Visit the [Penn Engineering faculty directory](http://www.cis.upenn.edu/about-people/index.php) to learn more about our distinguished faculty and their research areas.
