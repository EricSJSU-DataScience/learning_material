# Community Guidelines

One of the things we value greatly at Penn Engineering Online is our robust community of students, instructors and staff. We cultivate a strong, welcoming community that is respectful and supportive. Throughout your time in the program, you will be interacting with people through various forms of written and verbal communication. The following community guidelines are important to keep in mind when communicating online.

---

## Core Guidelines

**Tone**
Tone is a very important part of online communication. Before posting online, read your message out loud. Ask yourself if you would say this to a colleague or acquaintance in a face-to-face discussion. Avoid sarcasm, which can be hard to interpret online.

**Professionalism**
Make an effort to understand, respect, and support your fellow students. People have different perspectives—but everyone is here to learn and to learn from each other. As this is an educational environment, remember to keep all language, conversations, and exchanges professional and respectful.

**Confidentiality**
Do not share information about others and their experiences without their expressed permission to do so.

**Non-judgemental**
We want our students to feel free to pose questions or ask others to share their thoughts or expertise. There are many different backgrounds and experiences represented at Penn Engineering Online! Remember to be cautious in judging others for their thoughts, opinions, questions, or concerns.

---

## Communication Tools

The following communication tools will be used throughout the program. Keep in mind that the Community Guidelines apply in all of these settings:

- **Slack** - Peer-to-peer communication only*
- **Zoom** - Online meetings/office hours with faculty or TAs
- **Ed Discussion Forum** - Course related questions*
- **Email** - Student email addresses can be found on the "Classmates" page in every course
- **[Program's private LinkedIn group](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/connect-with-student-support/)**

*You will have the opportunity to practice using these tools during the onboarding course.

---

## Questions?

Now that you are an admitted student, there is a different email address for you to use to contact us. From now on, please contact the [student success team](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/connect-with-student-support/) (not the online-admissions email).

**Important:** Please include your Penn ID number in all email correspondence with the program and university.

---

## Feedback?

We welcome feedback from our students. Please see the Program Feedback section to learn the best ways to provide feedback for courses, event programming, and your experience at Penn Engineering Online.

If you need to discuss a private matter (health or personal issue), please sign up for your program's private office hours found in the [Student Knowledge Base](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/).

---

## Key Points

- Community values: respectful, supportive, welcoming environment
- Read messages aloud before posting to check tone
- Maintain professionalism in all communications
- Respect confidentiality—don't share others' information without permission
- Approach others non-judgmentally—diverse backgrounds and perspectives are valued
- Community Guidelines apply across all communication platforms (Slack, Zoom, Ed Discussion, Email, LinkedIn)
- Contact student success team (not admissions) for support; include Penn ID in correspondence
- Private matters can be discussed during office hours in the Student Knowledge Base
