# Slack Activity: Introduce Yourself

We want to meet you! This is your opportunity to connect with your classmates and start building relationships within the MSE-DS community.

---

## Step 1: Update Your Slack Profile

Add your program to your Slack profile so your peers can learn more about you.

1. Click on your profile icon in the top right corner of Slack
2. Select "Profile" from the drop-down menu
3. Next to your name, click "Edit"
4. Update your "Title" to include your program name (e.g., "MSE-DS")

---

## Step 2: Post Your Introduction

Please introduce yourself in the appropriate #introductions channel for your program:

- **#intros-mcit** - For MCIT students
- **#intros-mse-ds** - For MSE-DS students
- **#intros-cert** - For Online Graduate Certificate students

Feel free to get creative and write a response or post a link to a video of yourself!

---

## Introduction Topics

Here are some suggestions for topics to cover in your introduction:

- **Your name** - Start with the basics
- **Your occupation** - What do you do professionally?
- **Your location** - Where are you located?
- **Why you enrolled at Penn Engineering Online** - What motivated your decision to join the program?
- **What brings you joy?** - Share something personal and interesting about yourself

Feel free to include additional information that you think would help your classmates get to know you better!

---

## Engage with Your Classmates

We encourage you to read through your classmates' introductions and respond via the "Reply to thread" feature! This is a great way to:

- Start conversations with future study partners
- Build connections within your cohort
- Create a sense of community from day one
- Find peers with similar interests or backgrounds

---

## Key Points

- Update your Slack profile with your program name
- Post a thoughtful introduction in your program's #introductions channel
- Be creative—text, video, or multimedia introductions are welcome
- Read and engage with your classmates' introductions
- Use the "Reply to thread" feature to keep conversations organized
- Starting with introductions helps build the online community and makes classes more enjoyable
- Don't be shy—your classmates want to meet you too!
