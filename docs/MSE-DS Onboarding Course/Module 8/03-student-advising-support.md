# Student Advising Support

We know that Academic Advising may be new to some of you, and you may not be sure what to expect from having an Advisor, so here is some information that can help you know what to expect and how to best utilize our academic advisors. All students are assigned an academic advisor in Path.

## What do we do?

Our team's mission at Penn Engineering Online is to advocate for our students and empower them to succeed from their very first semester through graduation. Through email communication, advising appointments, and live virtual events, we aim to provide support and resources for students to persist through their academic programs and create a sense of community and belonging at Penn Engineering Online.

## What are we working towards?

Our vision as your academics team is to foster a supportive learning environment where all of our students have the tools to achieve their educational, personal, and career goals.

## How do we define student success?

Penn Engineering Online Academics Team values student success as our highest priority. Below is how we define student success:

- Encouraging a student's development of their holistic self as they take on their educational journey.
- Proactively supporting students with the resources they need to persist through graduation.
- Providing opportunities for students to engage with the Penn Engineering Online community and foster a sense of belonging.
- Building a culture of respect, empathy, and inclusiveness. We want every student to feel safe in their program.
- Inspiring and motivating students to continue to grow as learners within their studies.
- Empowering students to advocate for themselves and also to be reliable advocates for our students.
- Striving to be a solution-oriented resource for our students and keep our students satisfied with their program experience.

## What are our expectations?

| Expectations of Advisees | Expectations of Advisors |
|-------------------------|------------------------|
| Demonstrate respect and professionalism for others. | Provide an empathic and welcoming space. |
| Be the agent of your educational journey and take steps to plan for your degree. | Actively listen to our students and their needs. |
| Arrive to meetings prepared with questions and ready to take any necessary notes. | Be familiar with Penn Engineering Online and Penn resources. Know where to point students in the direction of help and support. |
| Professional email etiquette (including your Penn ID, a greeting, body, and sign-off). | Be transparent, and clear in our communications. |
| Be on time for all advising meetings and cancel meetings that you can no longer attend. | Be prompt to all student meetings and communicate with students if there is any delay. |
| Be familiar with the Penn Engineering Online handbook policies, Student Knowledge Base, and important deadlines. | Be knowledgeable and familiar with all Penn Engineering Online and Penn policies - including deadlines. |
| Use your SEAS email and check this email regularly to keep up with announcements and stay up to date with all program communications. | Responding within two business days upon receipt of student emails and promptly following up on any items that require any additional information. |
| Be mindful of your Zoom environment. Plan ahead to the best of your ability to be in a quiet area and have the Zoom link on hand prior to your meeting. | Have private and secure locations for all Zoom meetings, adhering to the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). |

## How to Contact Your Advisor

You can view all of the ways to contact our team for your specific program in the [Student Knowledge Base](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/). There you will find the specific links for each of the support services below:

**Email** - Based on your program, send an email for questions about degree planning, academic advising, or any question that you're not sure who to ask! It will be directed to the correct person.

**Individual Advisor Office Hours** - Face to face and over Zoom, your assigned advisor will meet with you to discuss more complex questions that may take more than 15 minutes. For example, academic standing or a delicate personal issue.
