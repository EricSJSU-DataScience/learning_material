# Tech Spotlight: Honorlock

Most online courses utilize proctoring for exams. This ensures that the exams remain secure and that everyone is testing under the same conditions and has an equal experience while taking that exam. We know that taking a proctored exam is a new experience for most people so we want to make sure that we provide you with as much information about it in advance as well as give you the opportunity to take a practice exam.

## Introducing Honorlock

Honorlock is an online proctoring service that allows you to take your exam from the comfort of your home. You DO NOT need to create an account or schedule an appointment in advance. Honorlock is available 24/7 and everything that you need is listed below:

- A Computer
- Google Chrome Browser
- Honorlock Google Chrome Extension
- A working webcam/microphone
- Your Penn or government-issued photo ID that matches your official name you provided to Penn and uses the Roman alphabet
- A stable internet connection.

## Getting Started with Honorlock

Please click here to view ["How to use Honorlock (Student Guide)" video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kI6dtREwO9o).

## Getting Ready

1. Make sure your [Google Chrome Browser](https://www.google.com/chrome/update/) is up-to-date
2. For Mac users, make sure you have enabled [screen recording](https://honorlock.kb.help/mac-os-1015-catalina-screen-recording/) for Google Chrome
3. You may only use one monitor while taking the exam
4. You must allow pop-ups and disable any pop-up blockers before you take the exam with Honorlock ([Instructions](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95472))
5. Open Google Chrome and download the [Honorlock Chrome Extension](https://honorlock.kb.help/install-the-honorlock-extension/)
6. To check if your device meets the minimum system requirements:
   - Please visit [Honorlock Support](https://honorlock.com/support/), scroll to the 'Minimum Requirements', and press 'Run System Check'
   - Run a [Network Speed Test](https://honorlocksupport.speedtestcustom.com/) to make sure you have a stable Internet Connection

## Start the Honorlock session

When you are ready to start the proctoring session, navigate to the quiz item within the module and agree to the terms of service. This will begin the Honorlock authentication process, where you will:

1. Take a picture of yourself
2. Show your photo ID
   - Penn or Government-issued photo ID that matches your official name you provided to Penn and uses the Roman alphabet
   - If you are an international student with a non-English government ID, chat with Honorlock Support to manually verify your ID

## After the verification process

1. Press the blue "start" button at the bottom of the screen to begin your exam.
2. The timer for your exam will now start and proctoring will end as soon as you submit your exam to Canvas.

Honorlock will be recording your exam session through your webcam, microphone, and recording your screen. Honorlock also has an integrity algorithm that can detect search-engine use, so please do not attempt to search for answers, even if it's on a secondary device.

## Proctoring Area Requirements

- Take your exam in a quiet, secure room with a door that can be closed/locked so no one walks in or talks to you during your exam
- The room should have good lighting that is "daylight" quality. Lighting should be overhead and/or in front of you; the light source should NOT be behind you.
- There should not be any other people (including children) in the room at any time during the exam.
- All other electronic devices in the room must be fully powered off during the exam (such as additional computer monitors, laptops, iPads, phones, etc).
- Nothing should be on/under the desk/table except the computer. All other books, papers, notes or other materials must be removed unless specifically permitted in written guidelines for a particular examination
- There should be no visible writing on the laptop, desk, or nearby walls that could be construed as notes or exam aids.
- Make sure your webcam and microphone are operational.
- Do not wear any hats, hoodies, sunglasses, or anything that obscures your eyes or face.
- Do not communicate with others by any means (phone, text, chat, etc.).
- Do not play any music or have the television on.
- Do not use any headphones.
- You will be allowed short (5-minute) bathroom breaks during the exam (for specified exams only).
- Have a proper photo ID (Penn or Government issued photo ID that matches your official name you provided to Penn and uses the Roman alphabet) to show at the beginning of each exam.

## Getting Support

Honorlock support is available **24/7/365**. If you encounter any issues, you may contact them through live chat on the [support page](https://honorlock.com/support/) or within the exam itself. Here are additional resources:

- [Student FAQ](https://honorlock.kb.help/honorlock-student-faq/)
- [Honorlock Knowledge Base](https://honorlock.kb.help/students-starting-exam/)
- Should you encounter an issue during your exam that cannot be resolved by Honorlock support, please send a private Ed Discussion post immediately. The Penn Engineering Online staff will be available between 8 am eastern and 10 pm eastern to respond to these posts

## After the Exam

After the exam, you can remove the Honorlock Extension from your Chrome Browser by following these [instructions](https://honorlock.kb.help/removing-the-honorlock-extension/).
