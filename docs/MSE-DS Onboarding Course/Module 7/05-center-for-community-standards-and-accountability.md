# The Center for Community Standards and Accountability

![alt text](05_image1.png)

Center for Community Standards and Accountability (CSA) reviews and investigates potential violations of the University's Code of Academic Integrity and Code of Student Conduct. Additionally, the CSA offers mediation services as part of its Conflict Resolution Program (via phone or Skype). Students who would like to contact the CSA can either [refer a case, request a consultation](https://csa.upenn.edu/community-standards/refer-caserequest-consultation) or call the office at 215-898-5651 during normal business hours (9am-5pm ET).

---

## CSA Services and Support

The CSA is available to consult and offer support to students who are navigating a policy violation concern. The CSA will accept all cases of alleged violations of the Code of Academic Integrity involving online students that occur within the Penn Engineering Online academic platform (e.g., Canvas, Slack, Zoom).

---

## Code of Student Conduct Violations

Additionally, the CSA will provide services, including case management and consultation, when there are alleged violations of the Code of Student Conduct. Examples of violations include:

- Inappropriate conduct that occurs within the Penn Engineering Online academic platform;
- Inappropriate conduct that occurs outside of the academic setting but still in a Penn or program context, whether the conduct occurs online or in person;
- Inappropriate conduct that occurs on campus, if applicable;
- Inappropriate conduct that occurs between two or more Penn Engineering Online students, regardless of location, will be evaluated by the Director of CSA on a case-by-case basis in order to determine CSA involvement.
- Extreme violations of the Code of Student Conduct that fall outside of the above mentioned parameters.

In general, CSA involvement will be determined on a case-by-case basis at the discretion of the CSA.
