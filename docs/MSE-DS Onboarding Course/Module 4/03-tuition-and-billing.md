# Tuition

Watch the most recent [Student Financial Services Information Webinar](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xr0-i7fWnpc&list=PLmm1MLbrTGam0Wbf4McxCxAXn6TqxAOt6&index=1)! Joseph Manning, Associate Director – Graduate & Professional Aid, from the Student Financial Services team, who discusses and answers questions about student Financial Aid and Penn Payment Plans for students this spring.

You will receive your tuition bill after you register for courses. Electronic bills or e-bills are sent from the University's Student Registration and Financial Services (SRFS) Office to the student's official email address on file. Your billing statement can then be accessed through [PennPay](https://srfs.upenn.edu/billing-payment/pennpay), which is where you will find the bill due date. Please note that you will receive a notification when a bill has been generated, but you will not receive a reminder when the bill is due.

Changes in registration after the second week of classes necessitate manual tuition adjustments. If you notice a mistake with your bill, please contact the [student success team](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/student-knowledge-base/connect-with-student-support/).

Ideally, a student's bill should reflect all charges and all financial aid credits for the semester in question. Frequently, University and departmental financial aid awards are credited after the date of initial billing. When this happens, the bill reflects a higher debt than the student anticipated. The student's first step is to check with their program to make sure the financial aid was credited. Students utilizing tuition benefits with questions should contact [tuition@hr.upenn.edu](mailto:tuition@hr.upenn.edu). Students supported from outside the University should check directly with their sponsors.

The University will withhold diplomas at graduation when financial questions are unresolved. It is the responsibility of the student to keep track of their outstanding balance and to resolve any problems that might arise.

## Tuition and Fees (subject to change)

### Enrollment Deposit

For students pursuing degree programs, the $300 deposit you paid when you submitted your enrollment form is non-refundable. A $300 credit will be applied to the tuition bill for the next semester that you register for courses. That is, the credit will not be applied to your current semester's tuition, but if you register for courses in the subsequent semester, it will be applied to that billing cycle.

**The most accurate tuition and fees can always be found here:** [https://online.seas.upenn.edu/degrees/tuition-financial-aid/](https://online.seas.upenn.edu/degrees/tuition-financial-aid/) 

Please be aware that tuition and fees are posted as a guide and may be subject to change. Students may also be required to purchase textbooks or supplies for a few of the courses in the program. Tuition will vary depending upon how many courses a student enrolls in each semester. 

Online students who are enrolled at least half-time* will be eligible to apply for [federal student loans](https://srfs.upenn.edu/financial-aid/apply/graduate-checklists). International students' eligibility may vary depending upon their international status. 

\*For Penn Engineering Online students, "half-time" is defined as taking at least two courses in a term.

\*\* Penn Engineering Online Certificate Students and Non-Degree seeking students DO NOT qualify for Federal Financial Aid.
