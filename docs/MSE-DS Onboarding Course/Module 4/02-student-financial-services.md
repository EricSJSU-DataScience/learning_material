# Student Financial Services Overview

Student Financial Services is here to help you manage your tuition payments and navigate financial aid options. This page provides guidance from Elaine Varas (Senior University Director of Student Financial Aid) and Erick Herrera (Deputy Director of Student Financial Aid).

---

## About Student Financial Services

Our goal is to make it easy for you to navigate the process of paying for your Penn education, so you can focus on incredible opportunities ahead of you.

Student Financial Services can assist you with:
- Understanding your bill and tuition charges
- Making payments on your student account
- Applying for financial assistance
- Managing your financial aid
- Understanding university financial aid policies

---

## Penn.Pay: Your Billing System

### What is Penn.Pay?

Penn.Pay is Penn's electronic billing system where you can view your full student account statement and make real-time payments.

### How to Access Penn.Pay

1. Log in to the **Penn InTouch portal**
2. Use your **Penn Key and password**
3. Access Penn.Pay from the portal

### What You Can View in Penn.Pay

- **Recent activity** - Recent transactions on your account
- **Latest statement** - Most recent bill
- **Monthly statements** - Generated each month
- **Tuition and fees** - Appear on your first bill each semester
- **Financial aid credits** - Display as credits on your account

---

## Understanding Your Bill

### When Bills Are Generated

- **First bill of semester** - Tuition and fee charges appear on your first bill each semester
- **Monthly statements** - Your statement is generated each month
- **Financial aid credits** - If you're receiving financial aid funds, they appear as credits on your account

### Charges on Your Bill

Your bill includes charges based on the rates of your specific program, including:
- Tuition charges
- Program fees
- Required textbooks and supplies (in cost of attendance calculation)
- Other applicable fees

---

## Paying Your Bill

### Payment Options

**Through Penn.Pay (Online)**
- Click "Make Payment" in Penn.Pay at any time before your bill is due
- Make payments in real time
- Receive immediate confirmation

**Authorize Family Members**
- If a family member will be paying on your behalf
- Add them as an **authorized user** on Penn.Pay
- They can then make payments directly

**Outside Penn.Pay**
- **Mail a check** to the Student Accounts office
- **Submit a wire transfer** (details available through Student Financial Services)

**External Sponsorship**
- If you're being sponsored by an external organization
- Contact the **Student Accounts office** for instructions

### Payment Plan Option

**Penn Payment Plan**
- Available prior to each semester
- Allows you to budget your balance into **four separate payments** throughout the semester
- Helps you spread costs over time

**How to Enroll**
- Learn more about enrollment criteria and deadlines on the [Student Financial Services website](https://www.srfs.upenn.edu/)
- Enrollment must occur before semester-specific deadlines

---

## Payment Deadlines and Consequences

### Important Deadlines

**Make payment before each due date** to avoid penalties and holds.

### Late Payment Consequences

- **Late fee** - Incurred if payment is not made by the due date
- **Financial hold** - A past due balance may cause you to be placed on financial hold
- **Registration blocked** - Financial holds prevent you from registering for classes

## Financial Aid and Funding

### Overview

If you need assistance paying for your Penn education, Student Financial Services can help you access financial aid funding.

### Applying for Financial Aid

**Step 1: Complete the FAFSA**
- Applicant: All students seeking federal financial aid
- Form: Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)
- Website: [FAFSA website](https://studentaid.gov/h/apply-for-aid/fafsa)
- Information collected: Family income, financial details, eligibility determination

**Step 2: Budget Development**
- Student Financial Services will construct a **cost of attendance** budget for you
- **Includes:**
  - Tuition
  - Fees
  - Required books and supplies
- **Represents:** Maximum amount of financial aid funding you can receive in an academic year

**Step 3: Determine Borrowing Needs**
- You can choose to borrow funding to cover all or a portion of your costs
- Student Financial Services will assist you in determining how much you may need to borrow

### Types of Financial Aid

**Federal Loans (Primary Source)**
- Available to US citizens and permanent residents
- Generally offer the best terms and repayment options
- Should be your first choice

**Private Funding (International Students)**
- Federal funding is not available to international students
- Student Financial Services can help you apply for private source funding

## Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

### What is SAP?

Eligibility for financial aid funding is based on satisfactory academic progress measurements.

### SAP Measurements

To receive federal financial aid, you must be progressing in your degree program and pass established measurements in:

- **GPA** - Cumulative grade point average
- **Completion rate** - Percentage of courses completed
- **Pace** - Progress toward degree completion
- **Maximum timeframe** - Time limit for degree completion

### SAP Assessment

- **Assessment timing** - End of each semester
- **Assessment by** - Student Financial Services
- **Possible outcomes** - May be failing one or more measurements

### If You're Not Meeting SAP

**Warning Notice**
- You may receive a warning notice if you're not meeting SAP

**Action Steps**
- Work with your **academic advisor** on course planning and study strategies
- Contact **Student Financial Services** to discuss your situation
- Take action to improve academic performance

**Continued Funding**
- You must address SAP concerns to continue receiving financial aid

## Enrollment Requirements

### Half-Time Enrollment Requirement

To continue receiving financial aid, you are **required to be enrolled at least half-time** in your program.

**At Penn:**
- Half-time minimum: At least **2 credit units** during a term
- Your program may have additional requirements

## Key Points

- Penn.Pay is the primary electronic billing system for viewing statements and making payments
- Bills are generated monthly, with tuition/fees appearing on the first bill of each semester
- Multiple payment options available: Penn.Pay, checks, wire transfers, and Payment Plan
- Pay before due dates to avoid late fees and financial holds
- FAFSA is required to apply for federal financial aid
- Cost of attendance determines maximum annual financial aid eligibility
- Satisfactory Academic Progress must be maintained to continue receiving aid
- Half-time enrollment (2+ credit units) required to maintain financial aid eligibility
- Student Financial Services staff available via email and phone for support and questions
